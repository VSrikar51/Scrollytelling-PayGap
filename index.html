Four Decades of Progress

Since 1980, women's earnings have risen from 60% to 83% of men's earnings.

But progress has stalled in recent decades.

The Stagnation

Notice how the line flattens after 2000. In the past 23 years, the gap has only closed by 9 percentage points.

At this rate, equity is still decades away.

At This Rate...

If progress continues at the current pace, it will take until 2070 to achieve pay equity.

That's 45 more years of inequality.

$400,000 Lost

A white woman with a bachelor's degree will earn approximately $400,000 less than her male counterpart over a career.

That's a house down payment, retirement savings, or a child's education.

The Compounding Effect

For Black women, the loss is nearly $900,000. For Hispanic women, over $1 million in lifetime earnings disappear.

This isn't just a gap—it's generational wealth denied.
